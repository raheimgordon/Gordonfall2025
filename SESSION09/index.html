The Art of Coffee

Exploring the rich culture and craft behind every cup

Welcome to Coffee Culture

Coffee is more than just a beverage. It is a global phenomenon that connects cultures, fuels conversations, and brings people together. From the highlands of Ethiopia to the bustling cafes of modern cities, coffee has woven itself into the fabric of human experience.

The History of Coffee

Ancient Origins

The story of coffee begins in the ancient coffee forests of Ethiopia. According to legend, a goat herder named Kaldi discovered coffee after noticing his goats became energetic after eating berries from a certain tree. The knowledge of these energizing berries spread across the Arabian Peninsula, where coffee cultivation began in earnest.

Global Expansion

By the 15th century, coffee was being grown in Yemen, and by the 16th century, it had spread to Persia, Egypt, Syria, and Turkey. Coffee houses became centers of social activity and communication. By the 17th century, coffee had made its way to Europe and then to the Americas, forever changing global culture and commerce.

Did You Know?

Coffee is the second most traded commodity in the world after crude oil. Over 2.25 billion cups of coffee are consumed every day worldwide. The word "coffee" comes from the Arabic word "qahwah" which means strength.
