How to Brew the Perfect Cup

  1. Start with Fresh Beans: Use freshly roasted coffee beans for the best flavor. Whole beans stay fresh longer than pre-ground coffee.
  2. Grind Just Before Brewing: Grind your beans right before you brew. The grind size should match your brewing method.
  3. Use the Right Water Temperature: Water should be between 195 to 205 degrees Fahrenheit for optimal extraction.
  4. Measure Your Coffee: Use the golden ratio of 1 to 2 tablespoons of coffee per 6 ounces of water, adjusting to taste.
  5. Brew for the Right Time: Different methods require different brew times. Pour-over takes 3 to 4 minutes, while French press needs 4 minutes.
  6. Enjoy Immediately: Coffee tastes best when consumed fresh. Avoid leaving coffee on a hot plate for extended periods.

Popular Brewing Methods

Pour Over: A manual method that gives you complete control over brewing time and water flow, resulting in a clean, flavorful cup.

French Press: An immersion brewing method that produces a rich, full-bodied coffee with more oils and sediment.

Espresso: A concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under pressure.

Cold Brew: Coffee grounds steeped in cold water for 12 to 24 hours, creating a smooth, less acidic beverage.
