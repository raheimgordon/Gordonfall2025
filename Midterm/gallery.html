Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is an important social ritual. Coffee beans are roasted, ground, and brewed in front of guests, creating a communal experience that can last several hours.

Italian Espresso Culture

In Italy, espresso is more than a drink. It is a way of life. Italians typically enjoy their espresso standing at the bar, quickly consumed as part of their daily routine.

Turkish Coffee Tradition

Turkish coffee is prepared in a special pot called a cezve. The coffee is finely ground and brewed with sugar, creating a strong, thick drink served in small cups with the grounds.

Vietnamese coffee, known as Ca Phe Sua Da, combines strong dark roast coffee with sweetened condensed milk and ice, creating a rich, sweet, and refreshing beverage.

Popular Coffee Varieties

Arabica

Origin: Ethiopia, now grown worldwide

Arabica beans represent about 60 to 70 percent of the world's coffee production. They have a sweeter, softer taste with notes of sugar, fruit, and berries. Arabica plants grow best at higher elevations and are more delicate than Robusta.

Robusta

Origin: Sub-Saharan Africa

Robusta beans have a stronger, harsher taste with a grain-like overtone and nutty aftertaste. They contain almost double the caffeine of Arabica beans and are often used in espresso blends for added crema and body.

Colombian Supremo

Origin: Colombia

Colombian Supremo is the highest grade of Colombian coffee. It is known for its smooth, well-balanced flavor with medium body and bright acidity. The beans are large and produce a clean, sweet cup.

Sumatra Mandheling

Origin: Sumatra, Indonesia

Sumatra Mandheling is known for its full body, low acidity, and earthy, herbal notes. The unique wet-hulling processing method gives it a distinctive flavor profile that coffee enthusiasts seek out.
