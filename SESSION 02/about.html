Introduction.

The oyster mushroom is a widely enjoyed edible fungus, known for its mild, savory flavor and velvety texture.

Key Features

  • Habitat: Grows on decaying hardwood logs and stumps.
  • Appearance: Fan- or oyster-shaped caps with white to cream gills.
  • Edibility: Popular culinary mushroom; nutritious and low in calories.

Fun Facts

  1. Oyster mushrooms are loaded with fiber, vitamins, minerals, and other important nutrients. They’re also low in carbohydrates, so they’re a good choice for people following low carb dietary patterns
  2. Oyster mushrooms provide antioxidants, which are substances that help reduce cellular damage in your body (4Trusted Source).
  3. Oyster mushrooms may promote heart health by reducing heart disease risk factors like high cholesterol and high blood pressure
